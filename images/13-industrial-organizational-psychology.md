# Figures: Chapter 13 — Industrial-Organizational Psychology

## Image descriptions for visual companions

1. **Hawthorne Works factory** — Historical photograph showing Western Electric manufacturing facility, Chicago ca. 1924. Caption: "The site of the Hawthorne effect discovery: observation changes behavior."

2. **Lighting levels and output graph** — Line chart showing paradoxical increase in output regardless of lighting improvement or decrease. X-axis: study phases; Y-axis: worker output; pattern shows increase, further increase, then sharp drop when observation ended.

3. **Three branches of I-O psychology** — Venn diagram or three-circle graphic: Industrial (hiring, assessment, training), Organizational (motivation, leadership, culture), Human Factors (ergonomics, tool design).

4. **Selection pipeline** — Flowchart: Job Analysis → Job Requirements → Assessment Tools → Candidate Assessment → Predictive Validity Check → Hiring Decision.

5. **Predictive validity comparison** — Horizontal bar chart: Cognitive ability r = 0.30–0.50; Structured interviews r = 0.37–0.50; Work simulations r = 0.37–0.50; Big Five r = 0.25–0.40; MBTI r ≈ 0 (crossed out).

6. **Herzberg's two-factor model** — Two-axis diagram showing hygiene factors preventing dissatisfaction (level off at neutral) vs. motivational factors creating satisfaction (rise toward high satisfaction).

7. **Expectancy theory formula** — Three multipliers: Expectancy (will my effort lead to outcome?) × Valence (do I value it?) × Instrumentality (can I control it?). Example: 0.5 × 0.9 × 0.1 = 0.045 (very low motivation).

8. **Job satisfaction vs. performance** — Scatter plot showing r ≈ 0.30 correlation (modest, substantial scatter, upward trend but many exceptions).

9. **Lewin's leadership styles** — Three parallel illustrations: Authoritarian (rigid, low when leader present/high when absent); Democratic (engaged, consistent); Laissez-faire (confused, low productivity).

10. **Transformational vs. transactional leadership** — Two-column comparison: Transformational (vision, inspiration, risk of charisma) vs. Transactional (clear goals, stable, less inspiring).

11. **Psychological safety in teams** — Two-panel: High safety (open dialogue, mistake admission, idea building) vs. Low safety (silent, hidden problems, playing safe).

12. **Organizational culture formation** — Circular diagram: Leader behavior → Stories, symbols, language → Employee beliefs and norms (bidirectional).

13. **The telework measurement problem** — Two-column: Easy to measure (wait times, backlogs) vs. Hard to measure (commitment, trust, psychological safety).

14. **Interconnected I-O systems** — Network diagram: Selection, Motivation, Leadership, Human Factors all linked to central Organizational Outcome node.
