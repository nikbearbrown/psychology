## Concept 1 — Emotion Theories

[FIGURE: Three theories of emotion compared. James-Lange: Stimulus → Arousal → Feeling. Cannon-Bard: Stimulus → Arousal AND Feeling (simultaneous). Schachter-Singer: Stimulus → Arousal → Cognitive Label → Feeling.]

[FIGURE: The amygdala's pathways. Fast path (thalamus → amygdala) for rapid fear response. Slow path (thalamus → cortex → amygdala) for detailed processing before emotion. Both can activate simultaneously.]

[FIGURE: Facial feedback hypothesis. Induced smile (pencil between teeth) increases positive emotion. Induced frown decreases positive emotion.]

## Concept 2 — Hunger & Homeostasis

[FIGURE: Hunger and satiety regulation. Hypothalamus integrates signals: ghrelin (stomach empty), low glucose, low leptin = hunger. High glucose, high leptin = fullness.]

[FIGURE: Ghrelin and leptin across the day. Ghrelin peaks when stomach is empty; leptin peaks after meals. Inverse relationship maintains homeostasis.]

[FIGURE: U.S. obesity prevalence trend, 1990-2020. Steep rise from 15% to 40%, suggesting environmental rather than purely biological causes.]

[FIGURE: Eating disorders prevalence. Anorexia and bulimia more common in White females from Western cultures. Overrepresentation in industrialized nations suggests cultural factors.]

## Concept 3 — Achievement, Sex, and Maslow

[FIGURE: Achievement motivation from stories. High-achievement narrative emphasizes goals and obstacles. Low-achievement narrative emphasizes relationships and leisure.]

[FIGURE: Overjustification effect. Before external reward: intrinsic motivation high. After payment introduced: intrinsic motivation decreases due to relabeling (*I do this for money*  instead of *for enjoyment*).]

[FIGURE: Testosterone and sexual motivation. Positive correlation across genders, but relationship is not deterministic.]

[FIGURE: Maslow's original pyramid. Five levels: Physiological → Safety → Love/Belonging → Esteem → Self-actualization. Note: empirical evidence does not support strict ordering.]

[FIGURE: Self-determination theory. Three needs (autonomy, competence, relatedness) → high intrinsic motivation.]

## Integration

[FIGURE: Three systems converge in emotion. (1) Limbic system rapid response (amygdala, fear/anger). (2) Autonomic arousal (heart rate, breathing). (3) Cortical appraisal (meaning-making). All activate simultaneously. Result: emotional experience + motivation to act.]
