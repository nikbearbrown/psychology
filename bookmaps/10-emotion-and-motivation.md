# Chapter 10 Bookmap — Emotion and Motivation

## Source Material Overview

The chapter synthesizes OpenStax Psychology Chapter 10, restructuring around three core concepts in Attenborough × Feynman voice (narrative-explanatory, scene-first):

1. **Emotion theories** (James-Lange, Cannon-Bard, Schachter-Singer, appraisal) with modern neuroscience integration
2. **Hunger and homeostasis** (ghrelin, leptin, obesity, eating disorders)
3. **Achievement, sex, and Maslow** (motivation frameworks, sexual orientation, gender identity)

---

## Key Revisions from Source

**Scene-first frame:** The cold open moves from abstract definitions to Ekman's 1967 Papua New Guinea fieldwork — a specific moment where universality was demonstrated.

**Mechanism over taxonomy:** Rather than listing emotion theories, the chapter shows how each theory captured part of the truth and how modern understanding synthesizes them.

**Multi-level causation:** Eating disorders are presented as requiring biological + psychological + cultural understanding, correcting the myth that they are "a choice."

**Sexual orientation clarity:** Explicit statement that conversion therapy is ineffective and harmful; biological evidence does not mean "unchangeable through willpower."

**Maslow critique:** The hierarchy is named as empirically unsupported; self-determination theory is offered as a better framework.

## Ideas for Authors

### Puzzles That Hook

- Ekman in an isolated culture recognizing universal facial expressions
- The Schachter-Singer epinephrine experiment (same arousal, different emotions depending on context)
- The overjustification effect (why adding payment to a loved activity can reduce the love)
- The snake in the backyard (why the same arousal feels different in different contexts)

### Specification Moves

- *Emotion* = arousal + appraisal + context (not synonymous with "bad feeling")
- *Homeostasis* = tendency to return to balance (not a fixed set point)
- *Intrinsic motivation* ≠ extrinsic motivation (not just different amounts)
- *Maslow's hierarchy* ≠ strict ordering (people pursue meaning despite unmet biological needs)

### Scale Shifts

- One facial expression → six universal emotions → billions of daily social interactions
- One hunger signal → complex homeostatic system → modern food environment disrupting it → epidemic
- One psychological need → framework for all human motivation

### Trade-Offs

- **Universal emotion vs. cultural display rules:** Same expression, different meanings
- **Homeostasis vs. environmental disruption:** Body defends set point, but the point shifts
- **Intrinsic vs. extrinsic:** Intrinsic more durable, but extrinsic can sometimes initiate behavior
- **Biology vs. choice:** Sexual orientation has biological foundations but is not chosen

### Misconceptions to Correct

- Emotions are purely biological
- You can read emotion from faces alone
- Eating disorders are a choice
- Sexual orientation is changeable
- Maslow's hierarchy is law
- Extrinsic rewards always motivate
