## Overview

**Audience.** This book is written for the undergraduate taking their first psychology course — typically a freshman or sophomore in their second semester, often fulfilling a behavioral-science requirement, often considering psychology as a major. They have heard pop-psychology claims their whole life. They are about to find out which of those claims is supported by careful research, which is partly true under specific conditions, and which is folklore.

## About the Author

**Nik Bear Brown** teaches at Northeastern University. His in-progress book *Intelligence?* — a natural history of cognition from bacteria to AI — sits at the intersection of computer science and psychology of cognition. His book *Irreducibly Human* asks which human cognitive capacities the AI era requires us to develop more carefully.

He is the founder of [Humanitarians AI](https://www.humanitarians.ai/) and the author of the *with LLMs* textbook series. His PhD in Computer Science is from UCLA.

He works in Boston. [nikbearbrown.com](https://www.nikbearbrown.com) · [irreducibly.xyz](https://irreducibly.xyz) · [skepticism.ai](https://www.skepticism.ai)
